A timeline of diplomatic summits with African states

This interactive map provides insight into the deepening diplomatic ties between African countries and their global partners. It depicts the growing number of countries that have hosted Africa+1 summits over time, from 1973 to today.

Read more

The term “Africa+1” refers to partnerships between African nations and individual non-African countries that convene at high-level summits to discuss areas of cooperation, including trade, security, and development.
A key finding is the shift in diplomatic focus toward Africa by various global powers over time. Initially, France was the primary host of Africa+1 summits, reflecting its close post-independence relationships with African countries from the 1970s through the 1990s. Around the early 2000s, countries in Asia, particularly China and Japan, began to invest more heavily in diplomatic and economic partnerships with African nations. By the 2010s, the landscape had diversified further, with the United States, Russia, and Saudi Arabia becoming prominent partners in Africa’s expanding international relations.
